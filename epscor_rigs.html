Research Initiation Grants
**All travel must be domestic only. Research Initiation Grant funds may not be used for international travel.

Oklahoma NASA EPSCoR will award up to four Research Initiation Grants (RIGs), each averaging $21,000. Travel Grant recipients who have been successful in establishing links with a NASA Center counterpart will be invited to compete for Research Initiation Grants. The purpose of these grants is to help develop research ideas and initiate projects related to NASA's Mission Directorates in collaboration with researchers at one of the NASA Field Centers. Please refer to NASA's Three Mission Directorates or solicitations from the Office of the Chief Technologist for areas of research interest.

In order to apply for a Research Initiation Grant, applicants must have been awarded a Travel Grant and have completed a visit to a NASA Field Center by December 7, 2012. These grants are primarily intended to support Post Doctoral Research time, Graduate Assistant(s), Undergraduate Assistant(s), materials and supplies, and travel to NASA Centers for extended research visits. In order to apply for a Research Initiation Grant, researchers must align the project with the 2011 NASA Strategic Plan and submit a single PDF document consisting of the following:

  1. A cover/application page (not included in page count).
  2. A research proposal limited to five pages of text. The proposal must address how the research will align with the Oklahoma's economic plan, an education agenda, and a plan showing steps that will be taken to develop student internships with partnering industries.
  3. The proposal must relate to one of NASA's Three Mission Directorates or Office of Chief Technologist.
  4. A one page budget with a justification for each line item (not included in page count).
  5. A two page vitae of the Principal Investigator (not included in page count).
  6. A letter of support and a commitment to collaborate from a NASA Center counterpart (not included in page count).
Only proposals utilizing the provided linked forms will be reviewed.

**Permanent equipment and faculty salaries may not be included in the budget.

Proposal criteria include the following:
  • Research Proposed:

    • Scientific and technical merit (25 points)

    • Relevance to NASA (25 points)

    • Relevance of the research project to Oklahoma's research interests and capabilities (10 points)

    • Plan for industry student internships (5 points)

  • Budget (15 points)

  • NASA Contact Letter - should reflect strong support for research collaboration (15 points)

  • PI Vitae (5 points)
Total Score (100 points)


Awards for Spring 2013 will be announced January 18, 2013.
