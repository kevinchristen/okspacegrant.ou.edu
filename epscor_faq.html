Frequently Asked Questions
Question: We see that a plan is required for student internships, but it isn’t listed on the cover/application page. Is the plan to be included IN the one-page abstract or is it an additional component. If separate, is it limited to a page?
Answer: One or two sentences within the abstract about the internship plan would be expected.

Question: Could you define “partnering industries”? Do you want to see private companies or does this include any federal agencies?
Answer: Within the CAN, NASA states that jurisdictions are “strongly encouraged to submit proposals that demonstrate partnerships or cooperative arrangements among academia, government agencies, business and industry, private research foundations, jurisdiction agencies, and local agencies”(Page Number 13, Beginning on line XXX. However, we have been directed by headquarters to focus on NASA research related industry(private companies), particularly within our state.

Question: Is it possible to guide undergrad students through NASA’s USRP program and grad students through the fellowship programs as an alternative?
Answer: No. Unfortunately, NASA’s USRP program for undergrads or fellowship programs for graduate students do not align with NASA directives for Travel Grants or Research Initiation Grants. Again, since these two steps are meant to help faculty submit successful Research Implantation proposals(as detailed in the appended CAN) it is expected internships are included in step one and two.

Question: What do you reasonably expect to see in these internship plans at the Travel Grant stage?
Answer: One or two sentences indicating that a company named in the abstract has agreed to the student internship. Although you did not ask this, undergraduate internships are recently being encouraged.

Question: What do you mean by the term internships? Internships will involve a university/industrial agreement and these can be troublesome as an intern would be considered a company employee rather than a student of the university (e.g., under a graduate assistantship).
Answer: It appears internships and requirements could have different definitions at colleges and universities across the United States involved in NASA EPSCoR/NASA Space Grant programs. It is important that each university follow the guidelines provided by their specific institution regarding internships.

Question: How is the budget going to support this idea or do you want to see this built into the RIG budget at the next stage?
Answer: We often receive Travel Grant proposals with a travel line providing funding for students to accompany the researcher to a NASA center. Depending on the proposal, this could be a way of demonstrating early involvement of students.

Question: Or do you expect that these partnering industries will be supporting the intern?
Answer: Travel Grants represent the first of the lock-step process. The purpose is for travel to NASA centers. Funding for internships would be reflected within the RIG proposal budget and budget justification. While matching funds are not required, within the RIG proposal, even marginal support by industry for these internships could be beneficial if a faculty submits a Research Implementation proposal.

Question: If a Travel Grant proposal does not have the internship component, will it still have a chance of reviewing well?
Answer: Yes.
